1. About this policy

This Privacy Policy explains how AXIO Advisory ("AXIO", "we", "us" or "our") collects, uses, stores, discloses and protects personal data when you visit our website, join our waitlist, request information, communicate with us or otherwise engage with our digital services.

AXIO is the data controller for the processing described in this Policy, unless we expressly state otherwise. Our registered address is [registered address]. You may contact us at advisory@axioadvisory.com or at the contact details in section 16.

This Policy is intended to support compliance with the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 and applicable guidance issued by the Nigeria Data Protection Commission. Where applicable, we also apply relevant requirements of the EU General Data Protection Regulation, the UK GDPR, the UK Data Protection Act 2018 and electronic-marketing laws.

2. Scope

This Policy applies to personal data processed through the AXIO website, waitlist and mailing-list forms, launch communications, website analytics, contact enquiries and related pre-client communications.

It does not govern personal data processed after a person becomes an advisory client. Client onboarding and advisory services may involve additional categories of data and will be governed by a separate client privacy notice, engagement terms and any applicable regulatory disclosures.

6. Waitlist confirmation and marketing communications

When you register for the waitlist, we may send an immediate administrative email confirming that the registration request was received or asking you to verify your email address. This confirmation or verification email is part of the service you requested and is not, by itself, consent to receive unrelated marketing.

We will send promotional or related advisory updates only where we have a lawful basis to do so. Where consent is required, the consent request will be separate, specific and optional. We will maintain evidence of the consent wording presented, the date and method of consent, the policy version and your subsequent preference changes.

Every marketing email will include a clear unsubscribe mechanism. Withdrawing consent or unsubscribing will not remove the minimum information needed to honour your suppression preference and prevent accidental re-enrolment.

10. Cookies and similar technologies

The website may use cookies, local storage, pixels or similar technologies. Strictly necessary technologies support security, load balancing, form operation, consent storage and fraud prevention. Non-essential analytics or advertising technologies will be used only in accordance with applicable consent requirements.

Where a consent-management interface is required, you will be able to accept, reject or adjust non-essential cookies. Withholding or withdrawing consent will not affect essential website operation. A separate Cookie Notice should identify each deployed cookie or technology, its provider, purpose, duration and category once the final production configuration is known.

11. Security

We use proportionate technical and organisational measures designed to protect personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure or access. These measures are expected to include encryption in transit, managed access controls, least-privilege permissions, multi-factor authentication for privileged users, environment separation, secure secret management, logging, backups, anti-abuse controls and incident-response procedures.

No system is completely secure. You should avoid sending sensitive information through ordinary email or website forms unless AXIO has provided an approved secure channel.

12. Your rights

Subject to applicable law and any permitted limitations, you may have the right to:

  • be informed about how your personal data is used;
  • request access to personal data held about you;
  • request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data;
  • request deletion of personal data;
  • request restriction of processing;
  • object to processing based on legitimate interests and object at any time to direct marketing;
  • withdraw consent at any time where processing relies on consent;
  • receive certain data in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format;
  • not be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing that produces legal or similarly significant effects, where applicable; and
  • complain to a competent data-protection authority.

To exercise a right, contact us using section 16. We may ask for information reasonably necessary to verify your identity and protect your data. We will respond within the period required by applicable law. Exercising a right is generally free, although the law may permit a reasonable fee or refusal where a request is manifestly unfounded or excessive.

13. Children

The website and waitlist are not directed to children under 18, and we do not knowingly collect their personal data through these services. If you believe a child has provided personal data, contact us so that we can investigate and take appropriate action.

14. Automated decision-making and profiling
